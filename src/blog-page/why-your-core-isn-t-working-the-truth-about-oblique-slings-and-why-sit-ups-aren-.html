Why Your Core Isn’t Working: The Truth About Oblique Slings (And Why Sit-Ups Aren’t Fixing It)

2026-04-27

You've done:

  • Core workouts

  • Pilates

  • Ab circuits

But you still feel:

  • Weak through your midsection

  • Tight in your lower back

  • Unstable when you walk, run, or lift

You might even notice:

  • One side feels different to the other

  • Your posture doesn't hold

  • Your body feels disconnected

This is where most people miss a critical piece:

Your core doesn't work in isolation. It works in slings.

What are oblique slings?

Oblique slings are cross-body systems that connect your:

  • Shoulders

  • Core

  • Hips

They allow your body to:

  • Transfer force efficiently

  • Rotate properly

  • Stabilise while moving

The two main ones are:

1. Anterior Oblique Sling (front chain)

Connects:

  • External obliques

  • Opposite-side internal obliques

  • Adductors

This sling helps with:

  • Rotational control

  • Deceleration

  • Front-body stability

2. Posterior Oblique Sling (back chain)

Connects:

  • Latissimus dorsi

  • Opposite glute

  • Thoracolumbar fascia

This sling is responsible for:

  • Propulsion (walking, running)

  • Force transfer through the body

  • Back-side stability

Why people search this (even if they don't know the term)

Most people don't Google "oblique slings."

They Google:

  • "Why does my lower back keep getting tight?"

  • "Why do I feel weak on one side?"

  • "Why won't my core activate?"

  • "Why do my hips feel uneven?"

  • "Why does my shoulder hurt when I run or lift?"

These are often oblique sling problems in disguise.

What happens when your slings aren't working

If these systems aren't coordinated, your body:

  • Loses efficient force transfer

  • Compensates through smaller muscles

  • Builds tension in the wrong places

Common outcomes:

  • Chronic tightness (hips, back, shoulders)

  • Poor posture

  • Recurring injuries

  • Feeling "disconnected" in movement

Why core exercises don't fix this

This is where most people go wrong.

They try to fix sling problems with:

  • Planks

  • Crunches

  • Sit-ups

But here's the issue:

These exercises:

  • Don't integrate upper and lower body

  • Don't train rotation properly

  • Don't transfer into real movement

So even if you get "stronger," you're not becoming more coordinated.

Real-world example: walking

Walking is one of the most important expressions of your oblique slings.

When your slings work:

  • Opposite arm and leg coordinate

  • Your body rotates efficiently

  • Force transfers smoothly

When they don't:

  • You overuse your lower back

  • Your hips feel uneven

  • Your shoulders tighten

And because you walk every day, this reinforces the problem constantly.

Why stretching makes this worse

If your slings aren't working, your body creates tension to stabilise.

So when you:

  • Stretch your hip flexors

  • Stretch your hamstrings

  • Stretch your back

You're often:

  • Removing tension without fixing coordination

  • Increasing instability

  • Causing the body to tighten more later

The real problem: lack of integration

This isn't a strength issue.

It's an integration issue.

Your body isn't:

  • Connecting upper and lower halves properly

  • Managing rotation efficiently

  • Distributing force across the system

What actually fixes oblique sling dysfunction

To restore sling function, you need to:

1. Rebuild rotational mechanics

Rotation is the foundation of sling function.

Without it:

  • Slings don't engage properly

  • Force can't transfer efficiently

2. Integrate upper and lower body

Not just training muscles, but:

  • Connecting lats to glutes

  • Connecting obliques to adductors

3. Train in patterns that transfer to real life

If it doesn't improve:

  • Walking

  • Running

  • Daily movement

…it won't stick.
